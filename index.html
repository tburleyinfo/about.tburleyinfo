Everyday we are dealing with more and more unstructured data than we've ever dealt with in human history. Moreover the world needs people who specialize in cleaning, feature engineering, modeling, and visualizing data to make practical use of it. I am proud to say that I am one of those people, and I am using my position as a Cognitive Software Developer at IBM to help businesses tackle the ever growing problem of big data.

Fidelity Investments

Financial institutions house ever-growing masses of transactional data from credit card purchases to in-house resource tracking. As a Systems Engineering Intern at Fidelity I was tasked with building an anomaly detection system for a computational resource tracking database. This tool gave the database team at Fidelity insights on different programs and applications using abnormally high resource consumption, and provided a baseline for predicting which applications might spike in the future.

Kellogg Institute For International Studies

Under the direction of Dr. Ernesto Verdeja and Dr. Pat Regan, we performed a pilot study of triggers of mass atrocities by analyzing different mass killing, genocide, and civil war cases within the last century and manually coding them into a dataset by their triggers.
